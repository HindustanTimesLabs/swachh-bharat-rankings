How to game the Swachh Bharat rankings

By Harry Stevens

11 May, 2017

The government’s rankings of the cleanest cities in the country, released last week as part of the Swachh Bharat campaign, had some surprising outcomes. “Is Varanasi really cleaner than Bhubaneswar?” my editor asked in an article earlier this week. Others have asked some version of the same question: How were these rankings produced? And is the methodology sound?

The rankings consider three broad categories of data: documentation provided by the cities, feedback from citizens, and independent cleanliness inspections. Intuitively, one might think that the independent inspections — which involve unbiased people observing how clean the city actually is — would be the most important source of data. But in the way the Ministry of Urban Development calculated the rankings, independent inspections are the least important.

Instead, 45% of the ranking comes from documentation provided by the cities, 35% comes from citizen feedback, and the remaining 25% is derived from the independent inspections.
